## Overview

### Purpose

This Crisis Management Plan establishes protocols for identifying, responding to, and recovering from crises that could threaten Terra Lumina's:
- **Reputation**: Brand damage, trust erosion
- **Operations**: Platform outages, security breaches
- **Financial Stability**: Investor runs, project failures
- **Legal/Regulatory Standing**: Compliance violations, lawsuits
- **Stakeholder Relationships**: Investor, developer, or partner conflicts

### Definition of Crisis

A crisis is any situation that:
1. Threatens significant harm to stakeholders (investors, developers, employees)
2. Could result in material financial loss or legal liability
3. Requires urgent response with high stakes
4. Has potential for negative media coverage or reputational damage
5. Disrupts normal business operations

### Guiding Principles

**Transparency First**: Be honest, direct, and timely in all communications

**Stakeholder Protection**: Prioritize investor and customer safety and interests

**Rapid Response**: Acknowledge within 1 hour, full response within 24 hours

**Unified Voice**: Single source of truth, no conflicting messages

**Learn & Improve**: Every crisis is an opportunity to strengthen

## Crisis Classification

### Crisis Severity Levels

| Level | Description | Examples | Response Time | Escalation |
|-------|-------------|----------|---------------|------------|
| **Level 1: Minor** | Low impact, contained, no media risk | Customer service complaint, minor website bug | 24 hours | Customer Support Lead |
| **Level 2: Moderate** | Medium impact, potential reputational risk | Payment processing delay, project underperformance | 4 hours | Head of Operations, CCO |
| **Level 3: Serious** | High impact, certain media coverage, regulatory concern | Data breach (small scale), platform outage >4 hours, SEC inquiry | 1 hour | CEO, CMT (Crisis Management Team) |
| **Level 4: Critical** | Existential threat, major stakeholder harm, widespread media | Large data breach, fraud, project collapse, regulatory action, investor fund loss | Immediate (15 min) | CEO, Board, All hands |

### Crisis Categories

1. **Technology & Security**
   - Data breach / cyberattack
   - Platform outage / downtime
   - Payment processing failure
   - Ransomware attack

2. **Financial**
   - Project default or failure
   - Fraud (internal or external)
   - Investor fund loss
   - Liquidity crisis

3. **Regulatory & Legal**
   - SEC investigation or enforcement
   - State regulatory action
   - Class action lawsuit
   - AML/sanctions violation

4. **Reputational**
   - Negative media coverage (investigative journalism)
   - Social media backlash
   - Executive misconduct allegations
   - Greenwashing accusations

5. **Operational**
   - Key personnel departure (CEO, CTO)
   - Major partner termination
   - Developer fraud or misrepresentation
   - Natural disaster affecting projects

6. **External Factors**
   - Economic recession affecting investments
   - Regulatory changes (e.g., crowdfunding rules tightened)
   - Competitor crisis affecting sector perception
   - Climate event damaging multiple projects

## Crisis Response Framework

### Phase 1: Detection & Assessment (0-1 Hour)

**Immediate Actions**:
1. **Confirm the Incident** (15 min)
   - Gather initial facts (what, when, who, extent)
   - Verify accuracy (avoid false alarms)
   - Document timeline

2. **Classify Crisis Level** (15 min)
   - Use severity matrix
   - Determine category
   - Activate appropriate team

3. **Activate CMT** (15 min)
   - Send crisis alert to core team
   - Convene emergency meeting (Zoom or in-person)
   - Designate Incident Commander

4. **Initial Stakeholder Hold** (15 min)
   - Pause public communications (if needed)
   - Alert Board Chair (for Level 3-4)
   - Prepare holding statement

**Deliverable**: Crisis Assessment Brief (1-page summary)

---

### Phase 2: Containment & Stabilization (1-4 Hours)

**Objectives**:
- Stop the bleeding (contain damage)
- Protect stakeholders (investors, customers, employees)
- Prepare initial public response

**Actions**:

1. **Technical Containment** (if applicable):
   - Isolate affected systems
   - Patch vulnerabilities
   - Preserve evidence (for forensics)

2. **Stakeholder Protection**:
   - Freeze transactions (if financial crisis)
   - Enable 2FA/password resets (if data breach)
   - Activate customer support surge capacity

3. **Legal & Regulatory**:
   - Notify regulators (if required by law, e.g., data breach within 72 hrs)
   - Engage external counsel (if needed)
   - Assess legal liability

4. **Initial Communications**:
   - Draft holding statement
   - Identify affected parties
   - Prepare FAQ for customer support
   - Alert key partners (payment processor, KYC provider)

**Deliverable**: Containment Action Plan

---

### Phase 3: Communication & Management (4-24 Hours)

**Objectives**:
- Communicate transparently to stakeholders
- Manage media and social response
- Execute recovery plan

**Actions**:

1. **Stakeholder Notifications** (prioritized):
   - **Immediately Affected**: Direct email/SMS (e.g., data breach victims)
   - **All Investors**: Email update within 4-8 hours
   - **Regulators**: As required (often 24-72 hours depending on incident)
   - **Partners**: Phone calls to key partners
   - **Employees**: Internal all-hands within 2 hours
   - **Public/Media**: Press release or statement (if needed)

2. **Communication Channels**:
   - Email (primary for investors)
   - Website banner (crisis update page)
   - Social media (Twitter, LinkedIn)
   - Press release (if media coverage expected)
   - Investor webinar (for major crises)

3. **Message Development**:
   - **Acknowledge**: Confirm the issue
   - **Apologize**: If appropriate, take responsibility
   - **Action**: What we're doing to fix it
   - **Assurance**: How we're preventing recurrence
   - **Availability**: How to get help/more info

4. **Media Management**:
   - Designate single spokesperson (CEO or Comms Lead)
   - Prepare talking points and Q&A
   - Monitor media coverage (Google Alerts, news aggregators)
   - Respond to media inquiries within 2 hours

**Deliverable**: Stakeholder Communication Plan

---

### Phase 4: Recovery & Resolution (24 Hours - 30 Days)

**Objectives**:
- Restore normal operations
- Rebuild trust with stakeholders
- Implement corrective actions

**Actions**:

1. **Operational Recovery**:
   - Full system restoration and testing
   - Resume normal business (if paused)
   - Monitor for secondary issues

2. **Stakeholder Engagement**:
   - Daily updates (first 3 days)
   - Weekly updates (next 3 weeks)
   - Investor Q&A sessions
   - Customer support metrics tracking

3. **Corrective Actions**:
   - Implement technical fixes
   - Policy and procedure updates
   - Employee re-training
   - Third-party audits (if applicable)

4. **Ongoing Communication**:
   - Transparency reports (weekly during recovery)
   - "What We Learned" post-mortem (public)
   - Commitment to improvements

**Deliverable**: Recovery Roadmap & Public Post-Mortem

---

### Phase 5: Post-Crisis Analysis (30-60 Days)

**Objectives**:
- Learn from the crisis
- Strengthen systems and processes
- Restore full stakeholder confidence

**Actions**:

1. **Internal Debrief**:
   - CMT retrospective (what went well, what didn't)
   - Timeline analysis (response speed, decision quality)
   - Communication effectiveness review

2. **Independent Investigation** (if needed):
   - Third-party forensic review (data breach, fraud)
   - Legal investigation (if litigation potential)
   - Regulatory cooperation

3. **Policy Updates**:
   - Update crisis management plan
   - Revise operational procedures
   - Enhance monitoring/controls

4. **Board Report**:
   - Comprehensive crisis report
   - Recommendations for governance changes
   - Budget for preventative measures

**Deliverable**: Post-Crisis Report & Improvement Plan

## Communication Protocols

### Internal Communication

**Crisis Alert System**:
- **Method**: Slack channel (#crisis-response) + SMS to CMT
- **Message Format**: "CRISIS ALERT: [Level X] - [Brief Description] - CMT convene immediately"

**Internal Updates**:
- **Frequency**: Every 2 hours during active crisis, daily during recovery
- **Channels**: All-hands email, Slack, emergency meetings
- **Content**: Facts, current status, what employees can/cannot say publicly

**Employee Guidelines**:
- **Do Not**: Post on social media, speak to media, speculate
- **Do**: Refer inquiries to Communications Lead, follow approved talking points
- **Support**: Provide employee FAQ, scripts for customer-facing staff

### Stakeholder-Specific Messaging

**Investors**:
- **Tone**: Transparent, reassuring, specific about financial impact
- **Channel**: Email (primary), webinar for major crises
- **Frequency**: More frequent updates than general public
- **Content**: Financial implications, fund safety, recovery timeline

**Developers/Partners**:
- **Tone**: Collaborative, solutions-focused
- **Channel**: Direct phone calls (key partners), email
- **Frequency**: As needed for operational coordination
- **Content**: Impact on projects, partnership continuity, support needed

**Regulators**:
- **Tone**: Formal, cooperative, complete disclosure
- **Channel**: Official filings, written correspondence, phone
- **Frequency**: As required by regulation (often immediate for material events)
- **Content**: Full incident details, corrective actions, compliance measures

**Media**:
- **Tone**: Professional, factual, no speculation
- **Channel**: Press release, spokesperson interviews
- **Frequency**: Initial statement, then as significant updates occur
- **Content**: Verified facts, company response, corrective actions (no speculation)

**Employees**:
- **Tone**: Honest, rallying, clear about expectations
- **Channel**: All-hands meetings, email, Slack
- **Frequency**: Very frequent during crisis (every 2-4 hours)
- **Content**: Full context, what they can/cannot say, how they can help

## Crisis Scenarios & Playbooks

### Scenario 1: Data Breach

**Triggers**:
- Unauthorized access to customer data
- Malware/ransomware attack
- Insider threat (employee data theft)
- Third-party vendor breach affecting Terra Lumina data

**Immediate Actions** (0-1 Hour):
1. **Contain**: Isolate affected systems, disable compromised credentials
2. **Assess**: Determine scope (how many users, what data types)
3. **Preserve Evidence**: Maintain logs for forensic investigation
4. **Activate CMT**: Level 3 or 4 depending on scale

**Notifications** (1-72 Hours):
- **Affected Users**: Email within 24 hours (or as required by state law)
- **Regulators**:
  - GDPR: 72 hours to supervisory authority
  - State laws: Varies (CA: "without unreasonable delay")
- **Credit Bureaus**: If SSNs compromised, offer credit monitoring
- **Law Enforcement**: FBI IC3 (Internet Crime Complaint Center)

**Communication Template**:
```
Subject: Important Security Notice - Action Required

We are writing to inform you of a data security incident that may have affected
your personal information.

WHAT HAPPENED:
On [date], we discovered unauthorized access to our systems. Our investigation,
with the assistance of [forensic firm], determined that the following information
may have been accessed: [specific data types].

WHO IS AFFECTED:
This incident affects [number] users who [specific criteria]. If you are receiving
this email, your account may have been affected.

WHAT WE'RE DOING:
- Engaged leading cybersecurity firm [name] for forensic investigation
- Notified law enforcement and regulatory authorities
- Implemented additional security measures including [specific measures]
- [If applicable] Offering [X] months of free credit monitoring through [provider]

WHAT YOU SHOULD DO:
- Change your password immediately (link provided)
- Enable two-factor authentication
- Monitor your financial accounts for suspicious activity
- [If applicable] Enroll in credit monitoring (instructions below)

We sincerely apologize for this incident. Data security is our top priority, and
we are taking extensive measures to prevent this from happening again.

For questions: security@luminousdynamics.org or call [number]

[CEO Signature]
```

**Recovery**:
- Forensic investigation (2-4 weeks)
- Implement security improvements (ongoing)
- Third-party security audit
- Transparency report on incident and improvements

### Scenario 4: Regulatory Investigation

**Triggers**:
- SEC inquiry or subpoena
- State securities regulator examination
- FinCEN investigation (AML concerns)
- CFPB consumer complaint investigation

**Immediate Actions** (0-2 Hours):
1. **Engage Legal Counsel**: Immediately contact external securities attorney
2. **Preservation Hold**: Suspend document destruction, preserve all records
3. **Internal Assessment**: Understand scope and potential exposure
4. **Board Notification**: Alert Board Chair and Audit Committee

**Communication** (Internal First):
- **Employees**: "We are cooperating with a regulatory inquiry. Do not delete
  any documents or communications. Refer all inquiries to Legal."
- **Board**: Full briefing within 24 hours
- **Public**: Generally do NOT announce unless inquiry becomes public

**Response Protocol**:
- Designate regulatory liaison (General Counsel)
- Provide requested documents promptly
- Coordinate with counsel on all responses
- Do NOT obstruct or mislead regulators
- Consider voluntary self-disclosure if violations discovered

**If Investigation Becomes Public**:
```
Statement: Terra Lumina is cooperating fully with [regulator] regarding
[general topic, if known]. We are committed to full compliance with all
applicable regulations. We have no further comment at this time.
```

**Resolution**:
- Negotiate settlement if violations found
- Implement remedial measures
- Transparent disclosure of outcome and changes

### Scenario 5: Executive Misconduct

**Triggers**:
- Harassment or discrimination allegations
- Fraud or embezzlement by executive
- DUI, arrest, or criminal charges
- Public statements damaging company reputation

**Immediate Actions** (0-4 Hours):
1. **Investigate**: Engage external investigator (law firm or HR consultant)
2. **Interim Action**: Suspend executive pending investigation (if serious)
3. **Board Engagement**: Emergency Board meeting
4. **Legal Review**: Assess company liability and employment law obligations

**Communication**:
- **Internal**: "We are investigating allegations regarding [name]. [He/she]
  has been placed on leave. We take these matters seriously and will act
  appropriately based on findings."
- **External**: Minimal disclosure unless publicly known
  - If media inquires: "We are aware of allegations and are conducting a
    thorough investigation. We have no further comment at this time."

**If Termination Occurs**:
```
[Company] announces that [Name], [Title], is no longer with the company
effective [date]. [Interim arrangement]. We remain committed to [values]
and will continue our mission without disruption.
```

**Succession**:
- Immediate interim appointment
- Formal search process
- Reassure investors and partners of business continuity

### Scenario 6: Social Media Crisis

**Triggers**:
- Viral negative post (e.g., "Terra Lumina is a scam")
- Coordinated attack or misinformation campaign
- Influencer or journalist criticism
- Customer complaint escalates online

**Immediate Actions** (0-1 Hour):
1. **Monitor**: Track mentions, sentiment, reach (use Brandwatch, Mention)
2. **Assess**: Legitimate grievance or misinformation?
3. **Respond or Not**:
   - **Respond if**: Factual error, legitimate customer issue, or rapidly spreading
   - **Do NOT respond if**: Troll, minor complaint, or likely to amplify

**Response Strategy**:
- **Acknowledge quickly** (within 1 hour)
- **Take conversation offline** if possible ("Please DM us your account details")
- **Correct misinformation** with facts and evidence
- **Stay professional** (never argue, insult, or be defensive)

**Example Response**:
```
Tweet: "We're sorry to hear about your experience. This doesn't reflect our
standards. Please DM us your details so we can make this right. [Link to support]"
```

**If Viral**:
- **Assess if broader statement needed**: If reaching mainstream media or
  significantly damaging, issue formal response
- **Engage supporters**: Alert brand advocates, investors who may defend organically
- **Paid response**: Consider promoted posts with corrective information

**Post-Crisis**:
- Review what triggered crisis
- Improve customer service or policy if legitimate
- Enhance social listening capabilities

## Stakeholder Management

### Investor Relations During Crisis

**Principles**:
1. **Over-communicate**: More updates, not fewer
2. **Honesty**: Disclose bad news quickly, don't sugarcoat
3. **Availability**: Provide direct access to leadership
4. **Transparency**: Share investigation findings, corrective actions

**Communication Cadence**:
- **Day 1**: Initial notification (within 4 hours of crisis activation)
- **Days 2-7**: Daily updates (even if "no new information")
- **Weeks 2-4**: Every 3 days
- **Month 2+**: Weekly until resolved

**Investor Support**:
- Dedicated crisis hotline/email
- FAQ page (updated daily)
- Investor-only webinars (weekly during crisis)
- One-on-one calls for major investors

**Managing Investor Panic**:
- **Freeze withdrawals** (if necessary and contractually allowed)
- **Provide reassurance** backed by facts (e.g., "Your funds are held in
  segregated accounts and unaffected")
- **Offer transparency**: Share financial health metrics

---

### Developer/Partner Relations

**Proactive Outreach**:
- Contact key partners before they hear from media
- Explain situation, impact on partnership, and continuity plan

**Reassurance**:
- Demonstrate business continuity (e.g., "All contracts remain in effect")
- Provide dedicated contact for partner concerns

---

### Employee Support

**Internal Communication**:
- More frequent, more honest than external
- Explain potential impact on company and jobs
- Provide talking points for customer-facing staff

**Employee Well-being**:
- Mental health resources (EAP)
- Town halls for Q&A
- Acknowledge stress and provide support

**Job Security**:
- Be honest about potential layoffs (if crisis threatens viability)
- Provide as much notice as possible

## Media Relations

### Spokesperson Designation

**Primary Spokesperson**: CEO
**Backup**: Head of Communications
**Specialized**: CTO (technical issues), General Counsel (legal matters)

**Training**: All spokespeople trained in media interviews, crisis messaging

---

### Media Response Protocol

**Inquiry Received**:
1. **Log inquiry**: Reporter name, outlet, deadline, questions
2. **Assess**: Can we respond? Should we respond?
3. **Prepare**: Draft response, get legal approval
4. **Respond**: Within deadline (typically 2-4 hours for news)

**Interview Preparation**:
- Anticipate tough questions
- Prepare 3 key messages (repeat in every answer)
- Practice bridging ("What's important to understand is...")
- Never say "No comment" (use "We're still gathering information" or "I can't
  comment on that specific detail, but I can tell you...")

**Key Messages** (Crisis Scenario):
1. **Acknowledge**: "We take this matter very seriously."
2. **Action**: "We have immediately [specific action]."
3. **Commitment**: "We are committed to [value/outcome]."

---

### Press Release Guidelines

**When to Issue**:
- Level 3-4 crises with expected media coverage
- Regulatory filings that are public
- Resolution of crisis (positive news)

**Structure**:
- **Headline**: Factual, not sensational
- **First Paragraph**: Who, what, when, where, impact
- **Second Paragraph**: Company response and actions
- **Third Paragraph**: Quote from CEO
- **Boilerplate**: About Terra Lumina

**Distribution**:
- Wire service (PR Newswire, Business Wire)
- Direct to key reporters covering fintech, renewable energy
- Post on website (newsroom section)
- Share on social media

executive names, "scam," "fraud," "data breach"

### Response Guidelines

**Respond When**:
- Factual inaccuracy spreading
- Legitimate customer complaint
- Question from influencer or journalist
- Opportunity to provide helpful information

**Do NOT Respond When**:
- Clear troll or bad-faith actor
- Minor complaint unlikely to spread
- Legal advice is to stay silent
- Responding would amplify issue

**Response Tone**:
- Empathetic and professional
- Concise (Twitter: make it fit in a tweet)
- Direct to resources (link to FAQ, support)

**Example Responses**:

| Situation | Response |
|-----------|----------|
| Factual error | "Thanks for bringing this up. To clarify: [fact]. More info: [link]" |
| Customer complaint | "We're sorry to hear this. Please DM us your details so we can help." |
| Misinformation | "This is inaccurate. Here's what actually happened: [brief correction + link]" |
| Support question | "Great question! [Brief answer]. Full details here: [link]" |

## Legal Considerations

### Legal Review of Communications

**Always Require Legal Approval For**:
- Regulatory notifications
- Admissions of fault or liability
- Financial impact statements
- Statements about ongoing litigation
- Privacy breach notifications

**Expedited Legal Review**:
- SLA: 1 hour turnaround for crisis communications
- Pre-approved templates for common scenarios
- Legal counsel on-call during crisis

---

### Privilege & Confidentiality

**Attorney-Client Privilege**:
- Mark communications "PRIVILEGED AND CONFIDENTIAL"
- Include attorney in investigation communications
- Avoid sharing privileged materials publicly

**Work Product Doctrine**:
- Investigation materials prepared in anticipation of litigation

---

### Regulatory Notification Requirements

| Regulator | Trigger | Timeline | Method |
|-----------|---------|----------|--------|
| **SEC** | Material event affecting securities | "Promptly" (case-by-case) | Form 8-K (if public company) or direct notice |
| **State Regulators** | Securities law violation | Varies by state | Written notice |
| **FinCEN** | Suspicious activity | 30 days from detection | SAR filing |
| **Data Protection (GDPR)** | Data breach | 72 hours | Online portal to supervisory authority |
| **State Data Breach Laws** | Data breach | 30-90 days (varies by state) | Written notice, online filing (some states) |

## Post-Crisis Recovery

### Trust Rebuilding

**Transparency**:
- Publish detailed post-mortem (what happened, why, how we fixed it)
- Share metrics (e.g., "We've reduced response time by 80%")
- Commit to ongoing transparency reports

**Accountability**:
- Acknowledge mistakes clearly
- Disciplinary action (if appropriate)
- Compensation for affected stakeholders (if appropriate)

**Improvement**:
- Visible changes (new CTO, new security features, policy updates)
- Third-party validation (audit reports, certifications)
- Ongoing communication of progress

---

### Reputation Monitoring

**Track**:
- Brand sentiment (social listening tools)
- NPS scores (weekly during recovery vs. baseline)
- Investor activity (withdrawals, new investments)
- Media mentions (volume and sentiment)
- Google search results (reputation management)
