## Executive Summary

## Direct Competitors

### 1. Mosaic (mosaic.com)
**Founded**: 2012 | **Headquarters**: Oakland, CA | **Funding**: $290M

**Business Model**
- Solar loan marketplace
- Connects homeowners seeking solar loans with investors
- Focus: Residential solar loans, not infrastructure projects

**Product**
- Loan investments: 4-6% returns
- Minimum investment: $25
- Loan terms: 5-20 years
- Platform fee: 1% annual servicing fee

**Strengths**
- 10+ years operating history
- Strong track record ($6B+ in loans originated)
- Established brand in solar financing
- Regulatory compliance proven

**Weaknesses**
- Limited to residential solar loans (not infrastructure)
- Lower returns (4-6% vs. Terra Lumina's 11-14%)
- No community ownership pathway
- No project equity, only debt instruments
- US-focused only

**Market Position**: Established player in residential solar financing, not direct competition for infrastructure projects

**Differentiation**: Terra Lumina offers equity in infrastructure projects with community ownership exits, higher returns, and broader project types (hydro, geothermal, wind, solar).

---

### 2. WePower (wepower.network)
**Founded**: 2017 | **Headquarters**: Lithuania | **Funding**: $40M (via token sale)

**Business Model**
- Blockchain-based renewable energy trading platform
- Connects energy producers with corporate buyers
- Focus: Pre-purchasing renewable energy at discount

**Product**
- Energy tokenization
- Smart contracts for energy purchase agreements
- Minimum: $1,000 equivalent
- Returns: 10-20% discount on energy prices

**Strengths**
- Blockchain technology for transparency
- Corporate buyer network
- International presence (EU, Australia)

**Weaknesses**
- Regulatory uncertainty (crypto/securities)
- Requires blockchain knowledge
- Not accessible to retail investors in US
- Complex token economics
- Platform mostly inactive since 2020

**Market Position**: Struggling; minimal activity since 2020, regulatory challenges

**Differentiation**: Terra Lumina uses traditional securities (SAFE, equity) with clear regulatory framework, accessible to US retail investors, no blockchain complexity.

---

### 3. Abundance Investment (abundanceinvestment.com)
**Founded**: 2012 | **Headquarters**: London, UK | **Funding**: $12M

**Business Model**
- UK-based renewable energy investment platform
- Debentures (bonds) in renewable energy projects
- FCA-regulated

**Product**
- Debentures: 4-8% target returns
- Minimum investment: £5 (~$6)
- Terms: 3-20 years
- Fee: 0.5% annual + 5% arrangement fee

**Strengths**
- FCA authorization (regulatory credibility)
- 10+ years track record
- £100M+ raised
- Proven bond structure

**Weaknesses**
- UK/EU only (not accessible to US investors)
- Debt instruments only (no equity)
- Lower returns (4-8%)
- Limited to UK/EU projects
- No community ownership pathway

**Market Position**: Leading UK platform, but geographically limited

**Differentiation**: Terra Lumina offers US market access, equity opportunities, higher returns, and community ownership model.

---

### 4. Trine (trine.com)
**Founded**: 2015 | **Headquarters**: Stockholm, Sweden | **Funding**: $12M

**Business Model**
- Impact investment in solar projects in emerging markets
- Focus: Solar energy access in Africa
- Debt-based loans to solar companies

**Product**
- Loans: 5-7% target returns
- Minimum: €25
- Terms: 3-5 years
- No platform fees for investors

**Strengths**

- €100M+ invested

**Weaknesses**

- Lower returns (5-7%)
- Higher risk (emerging market projects)

**Market Position**: Niche player in African solar financing

**Differentiation**: Terra Lumina focuses on developed markets (US, EU) with infrastructure projects, equity participation, and community ownership.

**Barriers to Entry**
- Regulatory compliance (high)

- Industry relationships (high)
- Brand/trust building (high)

**Terra Lumina Advantages**
- First-mover in specific niche
- Regulatory expertise (compliance-first design)
- Proprietary database (87K sites)
- Mission-driven brand differentiation

---

### 2. Bargaining Power of Suppliers (Project Developers): LOW

**Why**
- Large number of potential projects (87K+ dams, 4M+ sites globally)

- Platform provides value (access to retail investors)

**Terra Lumina Advantages**

- Low cost structure allows competitive fee rates

---

### 3. Bargaining Power of Buyers (Investors): MEDIUM

**Why**

- But: Limited alternatives for direct renewable infrastructure investment

**Terra Lumina Advantages**
- Unique offering (infrastructure + community ownership)
- Network effects (more projects attract more investors)
- Lower minimums than alternatives ($25 vs. $1,000+)

---

### 4. Threat of Substitutes: MEDIUM

**Substitutes**
- Green bonds (lower returns, lower risk)
- ETFs (liquidity, diversification)
- Community solar (no capital required)
- Direct property solar (for homeowners)

**Terra Lumina Advantages**
- Higher returns than bonds/ETFs
- Ownership vs. subscription (community solar)
- Lower barriers than direct property investment
- Mission-driven impact

### 5. Industry Rivalry: LOW (Currently)

**Why**
- No direct competitors in renewable infrastructure crowdfunding
- Indirect competitors focus on different niches
- Market growing rapidly (room for multiple players)

## SWOT Analysis

### Strengths
1. **First-Mover**: Unique positioning in renewable infrastructure crowdfunding
2. **Data Advantage**: Proprietary database of 87K+ untapped sites
3. **Mission Alignment**: Sacred Reciprocity License, community ownership model
4. **Regulatory Clarity**: Traditional securities framework
5. **Tech-Forward**: AI-powered discovery and matching
6. **Scalable Model**: Software platform, high margins
7. **Market Timing**: Perfect moment (climate urgency + retail investment boom)

### Weaknesses
1. **Brand Unknown**: New entrant vs. established platforms
2. **No Track Record**: Unproven model, no historical returns
3. **Regulatory Risk**: Securities regulations could change
4. **Complex Education**: Requires investor education on infrastructure investing
5. **Long Hold Periods**: 7-year exit model (illiquidity concern)
6. **Small Team**: Pre-Seed stage, limited resources

### Opportunities
1. **Market Size**: $2.3T global renewable investment market
2. **Regulatory Tailwinds**: Government incentives (IRA, ITC, PTC)
3. **Climate Urgency**: Massive capital needed for transition
4. **Retail Investment Boom**: Democratization trend
5. **Untapped Assets**: 87K dams, 4M+ global sites
6. **White-Label**: License platform to other regions/sectors
7. **Institutional Capital**: Partner with VCs/PE for large deals
8. **International Expansion**: Replicate model globally

### Threats
1. **Well-Funded Entrants**: Fundrise, Republic could enter space
2. **Regulatory Changes**: New securities regulations could increase compliance costs
3. **Economic Downturn**: Retail investment appetite could decline
4. **Project Failures**: Early project failures could damage reputation
5. **Fee Compression**: Competition could drive down platform fees
6. **Traditional Finance**: Banks/VCs could improve retail access
7. **Technology Risk**: Platform security breach or downtime
